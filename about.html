01

Practice

Zhou Heyang is an artist and designer whose practice moves across jewelry, object-based work, installation, and spatial research. Trained in metal art and design, his work examines how familiar objects, material systems, and spatial arrangements carry social meanings and invisible power relations.

02

Method

His projects often begin with ordinary materials — domestic leftovers, measurement symbols, pharmaceutical forms, architectural elements, clothing, and everyday gestures — and reorganize them into evidence-like systems. Through selection, transformation, and recontextualization, these materials become tools for observing family structures, symbolic value, bodily thresholds, and public space.
